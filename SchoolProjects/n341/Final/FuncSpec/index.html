One World Market - Functional Spec

Objective

One World Market is a Japanese grocery store that currently does not have its own website. This will be an attempt to design a website designed around customer needs, accessibility, and ease of use. The site will provide information on the types of products available. The goal is to increase revenue by improving new and returning customer interactions with the busines.

The success of the site will be measured by daily page views. Holiday periods will gain more and cause inflated results, but the average page view per month can be recorded and a trend can be drawn from the data.

Needs Analysis

The site will do the following:

  • Provide hours of operation
  • Showcase the products available
  • Display the menu
  • Provide directions to the store
  • Have a form to send special offers and information
  • Inform on holiday openings/closings of the business
  • Have content in both japanese and english where possible
  • Provide contact information

This information should be easily accessible and visually stand out. It should also display the quality of the service and its products, while not being cluttered by information and photos. It should cater equally and adapt to the needs of both the Japanese and American communities in terms of the information provided.

Audience Characteristics

  • New Customers
  • Returning Customers

Although the two groups can also be divided into two subgroups (American and Japanese), their purposes for visiting/researching the site will generally be the same: times, location, products, and news. New customers will be looking more for the latter two (times and location), while returning customers may focus more on the products (menu and items) and specific events/holidays.

The site will be geared towards pc users, but should support mobile users as well. There are few japanese stores within the Indianapolis, and some people come from far away just to shop at this place. Having mobile support should make it easier to look up information during travel.

Primary Audience

Primarily, the site will be geared towards japanese adults who will make use of the grocery portion of the store. While there are obviously more americans in the area, the vast majority are returning japanese customers who want access to their native products. Many products that are "necessary" in order to make traditional, every-day japanese food, and the store offers various brand options that other international stores do not have. These are people that will be looking for a genuine, focused shopping experience, not an off-brand isle of scattered asian products in an international supermarket.

The site should show the availability of such products, as well as inform the returning customer of any holiday(s) that would lead to scheduled closings or hour changes. There should be an option (ideally auto-detect) to switch the content to japanese for ease-of-access.

Secondary Audience

The remaining audience will be americans of all ages. This is due to the fact that there is a wide variety of products available.Children would be interested in Japanese candy, while adults may just be looking for healthier alternatives, or simply acquired a taste for japanese cuisine.

It will be necessary to display representative products as photos for these customers, as they may have little to no prior knowledge of what the grocery has to offer.
